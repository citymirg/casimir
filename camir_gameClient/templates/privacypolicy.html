How We Use Your Information

Firstly, your information enables us to operate the features and functionality of the Spot The Odd Song Out game. This includes the highscore tables and gameplay experience, e.g. creating artificial players or choosing human game partners for you. Apart from this, we do not use your personal information for any other purposes.

Secondly, we use your non-personal information in research to train music similarity models at City University. Especially your demographic and location information helps us understand how different groups of people perceive music similarity. Following termination of your usage of Spot the Odd Song Out, we may retain and continue to use your non-personal information in de-identified form for purposes of research.

Thirdly, we use your game-usage and non-personal information data in anonymised form for generating the public music similarity dataset STOSO as described below.

How We Share Your Information

Personal Information: Only your first name becomes available to other players of the Spot the Odd Song Out game, in order to identify you in the highscore tables and to other players who share a match with you. Your score may be sent to your friends in form of a Facebook achievement update. Apart from this, we will not share other personal information with anyone.

The STOSO Dataset The non-personal data will be used to create a freely available dataset intended for use in music similarity research. We are taking great care to anonymise your data, keeping the dataset free from any possibly personally identifiable information. The dataset will contain your age range (in 5 years spans), country, city and gender. This will be linked to game play data such as votes for the "Odd Song Out". Furthermore we may include anonymised information derived from your non-personal information, such as musical genres related to your music likes.

Except as otherwise described in this Privacy Policy, we will not disclose personal information to any third party unless required to do so by law or subpoena or if we believe that such action is necessary to (a) conform to the law, comply with legal process served on us or our affiliates, or investigate, or take action regarding suspected or actual illegal activities;
